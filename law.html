MY JOURNEY THROUGH LAW

As I was approaching the end of my honours undergraduate degree in mathematics and philosophy at Simon Fraser University, I decided to turn away from academia and pursue my long standing interest in social justice by attending law school. As a result of my LSAT score, I recieved a flood of applications from US law schools, including, much to my surprise, the highly prestegeous Columbia University. Not one to turn away from a challenge, I moved to New York City and completed my JD there. In my second year, I was named a Kent Scholar, the highest academic honours granted by Columbia.


While I did very well in law school, the practice of law has not been a great fit for my particular personality and talents. I struggled to develop a traditional legal career, spending several years working as a litigator for the Canadian Department of Justice, briefly worked in legal aid, then did a short stint at a boutique tax firm. I also spent some years teaching legal studies at Capilano University, a small teaching university in North Vancouver. Eventually, I returned to academia, and finished my PhD in law at the University of British Columbia in May 2023.


Moving forward, I hope to use my legal education and experience working in an innovative, co llabaorative, and intellectually stimulating environment that prioritizes helping society and producing concrete results.
