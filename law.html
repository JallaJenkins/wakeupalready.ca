MY JOURNEY THROUGH LAW

As I was approaching the end of my honours undergraduate degree in mathematics and philosophy at Simon Fraser University, I decided to turn away from academia and attend law school as a way to pursue my long-standing interest in social justice. As a result of my LSAT score, I recieved a flood of applications from US schools, including, much to my surprise, the highly prestegeous Columbia University. Not one to turn away from a challenge, I moved to New York City and completed my JD there. In my second year, I was named a Kent Scholar, the highest academic honours granted by Columbia.


While I did very well in law school, working as a lawyer challenged my personality and talents. I struggled to develop a traditional legal career, practucing for several years as a litigator for the Canadian Department of Justice, briefly working in legal aid, and then doing a short stint at a boutique tax firm. I also spent a few years on the faculty of legal studies at Capilano University, a small teaching university in North Vancouver. Eventually, I returned to academia, and finished my PhD in law at the University of British Columbia in May 2023.


Moving forward, I am looking to move away from the traditional legal world and find a new career path that builds on my knowlegdge and experience in a way that is compassionate, collaborative, and innovative.

CAREER HIGHLIGHTS


  • Graduated as a Kent Scholar from Columbia Law School in 1997.
  • Clerked for the BC Court of Appeal for the 1998-1999 term.
  • Litigated for the Canadian Department of Justice from 2000-2005 in the tax law, indigenous law, and criminal law sections.
  • Taught as a full-time faculty member in the Legal Studies Department at Capilano Univeristy from 2009-2015.
  • Completed an LLM in 2015 at the Univeristy of British Columbia, writing a thesis on moral philosophy and condominium law.
  • Completed a PhD in Law in 2023 at the Univeristy of British Columbia, writing a dissertation on mortgage-backed securities in Canada.
