A MATHEMATICAL MIND

I have always been fascinated by complex, abstract structures. As a toddler I taught myself to read so that I could do math problems. I first wrote code in the early 80s, when at the age of 10 I taught myself BASIC on an old Radio Shack computer using only a reference manual. I went on to complete an honours undergraduate degree in philosophy and mathematics and considered pursuing computer science before veering into law.


While practicing as a laywer, I dabbled in computer languages occassionally, learning the basics of HTML and C. But the COVID-19 pandemic spurred me to dive seriously into coding. During isolation, I learned HTML/CSS/JavaScript, Python, some Java and Lua, and Git/GitHub. More recently I have taken up C++, the PyQt/Pyside UI package, and Ruby on Rails.


Regardless of where my career takes me, I plan to continue coding as a hobby. However, I am excited about the possibility of folding mathematical analysis and software engineering into my future employment.
